Practical Electricity

Master the formulas and relationships used to compute electrical power, energy transfer, and cost.

Electrical Power

P = V × I

Unit: Watt (W)

Power is the rate at which electrical energy is transferred by a circuit.

V: Potential Difference / Voltage (V)
I: Current (A)
Energy Transferred

E = P × t = V × I × t

Units: Joule (J) or kWh

Total electrical energy transferred over an operating duration.

t: Operating time in seconds (for Joules) or hours (for kWh)
Cost of Usage

Cost = E(kWh) × Tariff

Unit: Dollars ($)

The financial cost of operating an appliance based on energy consumed in kilowatt-hours.

Tariff: Cost rate per kWh ($/kWh)

Energy Conversion Rule

E (Joules) SI Unit
Divide by 3,600,000
E (kWh) Billing Unit

Electricity billing uses kilowatt-hours (kWh) because the Joule (J) is too small, resulting in impractical, massive numbers. (1 kWh = 1,000 W × 3,600 s = 3,600,000 J = 3.6 × 106 J).

Appliance Power Benchmarks

Air Conditioner

~1000 W

High electrical energy draw due to compressor load. Used for long hours.

Electric Kettle

~2000 W

Very high electrical power consumption to change state rapidly. Used for short durations.

LED Desk Bulb

~10 W

Extremely efficient light output. Low electrical power draw.
