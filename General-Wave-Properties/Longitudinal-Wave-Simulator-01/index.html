Understanding Longitudinal Waves (Syllabus Objectives)

What is a Longitudinal Wave?

A wave in which the vibration of the medium's particles is parallel to the direction of energy transfer (wave propagation).

Compressions & Rarefactions
  • Compression (C): Particles are closest together; high pressure. Represents a displacement node.
  • Rarefaction (R): Particles are furthest apart; low pressure.
Medium and Wave Speed (O-Level Classic!)

Sound travels fastest in solids and slowest in gases due to differences in intermolecular spacing and elasticity.

Note: Frequency never changes when changing media. Thus, a faster speed in solids creates a longer wavelength!

Wavelength (λ)

The distance between the centers of two consecutive compressions or two consecutive rarefactions.
