Step-by-Step Worked Explanation

1

What does the slope represent?

On a Distance-Time graph, the gradient/slope represents the speed of the object. On a Displacement-Time graph, the slope represents the velocity.

2

Is the slope constant or changing?

Look closely at the line:
• Straight line = constant gradient (uniform rate)
• Curved line = changing gradient (non-uniform rate)
• Horizontal line = zero gradient (slope = 0)

3

What does that tell us about the motion?

• A zero slope means speed/velocity is 0, so the object is at rest.
• A positive constant slope means the object is moving forward at a constant speed/velocity.
• A curve bending upwards (steeper slope) means the speed/velocity is increasing (accelerating).
• A curve bending downwards (flatter slope) means the speed/velocity is decreasing (decelerating).
