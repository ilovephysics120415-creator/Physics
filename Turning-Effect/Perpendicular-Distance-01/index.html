O-Level Physics Masterclass: Perpendicular Distance

Perpendicular Distance vs. Arm Length

In physics, the moment of a force is not just Force × Distance. The distance must be the perpendicular distance from the pivot to the force's line of action.

Diagram A: Direct Case (90°)

Force is applied perpendicular to the lever arm. Here, perpendicular distance matches the arm length exactly.

Diagram B: Angled Case (< 90°)

Force is applied at an angle. The perpendicular distance is the shortest line from pivot to the dashed line of action.

Live Calculations

Perpendicular Distance (d): d = L × sin(θ) 1.0m × sin(90°) = 1.00 m
Moment (M): M = F × d 20 N × 1.00 m = 20.0 Nm
