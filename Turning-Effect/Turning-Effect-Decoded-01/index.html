Syllabus Hack: Perpendicular Distance

The distance d is measured horizontally from the pivot to the vertical force vector. If the force is applied directly *at* the pivot, $d = 0\text{m}$, creating zero moment!

Concept 1 Formula Checklist

Moment of a Force Equation

The turning effect of a force depends on both how hard you push (Force) and where you push (Distance from pivot).

M = F × d Standard Equation
• M = Moment of Force in Newton-metres (N m)
• F = Applied Force in Newtons (N)
• d = Perpendicular distance to pivot in metres (m)
Concept 2 Perpendicular Trap

Watch out for the "Perpendicular" Trap! ⚠️

Cambridge examiners *love* setting traps where the force direction is skewed. "d" must always be at a 90-degree angle to the force's line of action from the pivot.

⚠️ Tip: If the force's line passes straight through the pivot itself, distance $d = 0$, meaning the moment is exactly zero. You can't open a door by pushing on its hinge!
Concept 3 Direction Hacks

Pen / Ruler Pencil Hack

Unsure of direction in exam paper? Follow this 3-step physical hack during your papers:

  1. Place your physical pen/ruler flat onto the exam page directly over the beam graphic.
  2. Hold down your pen firmly at the spot where the Pivot is.
  3. Use your other hand to push the pen in the direction of the Force arrow.
  4. Watch how your pen spins. Simple!
