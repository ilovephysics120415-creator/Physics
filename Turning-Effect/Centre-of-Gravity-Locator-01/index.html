Centre of Gravity Locator

Turning Effect of Forces

Concept Introduction

Key takeaways

  • Every tiny particle in an object experiences a force of gravity pulling it down.
  • Instead of drawing hundreds of small forces, we can simplify: the entire weight acts from **one single point** called the **centre of gravity (CG)**.
  • The CG is represented with a dot and the letter G.

Key takeaways

  • If supported **exactly** at the CG, the object balances because the gravitational forces on either side balance each other out (net moment is zero).
  • If supported **off** its CG, gravity creates a turning effect (moment) around the pivot, causing it to tilt and fall.
