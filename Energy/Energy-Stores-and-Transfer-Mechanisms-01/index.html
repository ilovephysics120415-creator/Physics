Energy Stores & Transfers

In the O-Level Physics syllabus, energy is kept in six stores and shifted between them using four transfer mechanisms.

The 6 Energy Stores
⚡
Energy in kinetic store
Found in any moving object (e.g. running student, moving car).
🌍
Energy in gravitational potential store
Stored in objects raised above the ground (e.g. a book on a high shelf).
🧪
Energy in chemical potential store
Stored in chemical bonds (e.g. dry cell batteries, food, fuel).
🏹
Energy in elastic potential store
Stored in stretched or compressed materials (e.g. compressed spring, rubber band).
🔥
Energy in internal store
Total kinetic and potential energy of particles in a system (associated with temperature).
⚛️
Energy in nuclear store
Stored in the nucleus of atoms (e.g. uranium fuel rods, Sun's fusion center).
The 4 Transfer Mechanisms
⚙️
Mechanical
When a force acts on an object and moves it over a distance.
🔌
Electrical
When an electric current flows around a complete circuit.
🌡️
Heating
Due to a temperature difference (conduction, convection, radiation).
🌊
Wave Propagation
Transferred via waves, including electromagnetic (light) or sound waves.
