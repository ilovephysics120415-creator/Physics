Energy Transfers & Efficiency

Understand Sankey diagram mechanics and calculations under the O-Level Syllabus.

Sankey Diagram Principles

  • Width of each arrow is directly proportional to the amount of energy in Joules (J).
  • Input arrow enters from the left (representing the initial energy store/transfer).
  • Useful Output arrow continues to the right (representing the intended energy store/transfer).
  • Non-Useful Output exits downwards, always labelled as "energy transferred to internal store".
  • Conservation of energy: Total Input Energy = Total Output Energy (all stores combined).

The Efficiency Formula

Efficiency = Useful Energy OutputTotal Energy Input × 100%

Efficiency is expressed as a percentage (%) and can never exceed 100% due to unavoidable energy transfers to the internal store (e.g. via friction or resistance).
