Evaporation vs Boiling

Boiling

Occurs throughout the liquid at a fixed temperature (boiling point). This process requires a continuous external heat source to overcome intermolecular forces of attraction.

Evaporation

Occurs only at the surface of the liquid and at any temperature below the boiling point. The escaping energetic particles leave behind slower particles, causing cooling of the remaining liquid.

Phase Transition

Both represent a change in state from liquid to gas. However, their mechanisms, location of occurrence, temperature requirements, and thermodynamic effects are distinct.
