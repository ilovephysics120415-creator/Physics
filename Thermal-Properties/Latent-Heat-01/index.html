Latent Heat

O-Level Physics (Syllabus 6091)

Phase Changes

Melting, solidification, boiling, and condensation are processes where thermal energy is transferred without any change in temperature.

Latent Heat

Latent heat is the energy absorbed or released during a change of state at a constant temperature.

Specific Latent Heat L

The thermal energy required to change the state of 1 kg of a substance without any change in temperature.

Formula: Q = mL

Q = thermal energy (J) • m = mass (kg) • L = specific latent heat (J/kg)

Fusion vs. Vaporisation

Specific Latent Heat of Fusion (Lf): Solid ↔ Liquid transition.

Specific Latent Heat of Vaporisation (Lv): Liquid ↔ Gas transition.
