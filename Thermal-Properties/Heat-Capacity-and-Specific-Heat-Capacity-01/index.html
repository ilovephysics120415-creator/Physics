Core Definitions

Syllabus Quick Reference

Thermal Energy Q Joules (J)
Mass m Kilograms (kg)
Temp Change ΔT Degree Celsius (°C)
Relation C = mc
Q : Heat / Thermal Energy supplied
m : Mass of the substance
T : Temperature
Δ : Delta (representing "change in" a quantity)

Internal Energy

An energy store made up of the total kinetic energy (due to motion of particles) and total potential energy (due to intermolecular forces) of particles in a system.

Energy Store

Heat Capacity C

The amount of thermal energy needed to raise the temperature of an object by 1°C (or 1 K) without a change of state.

Q = CΔT
Unit: J/°C or J/K

Specific Heat Capacity c

The amount of thermal energy needed to raise the temperature of 1 kg of a substance by 1°C (or 1 K) without a change of state.

Q = mcΔT
Unit: J/(kg°C) or J/(kg K)
