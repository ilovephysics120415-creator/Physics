Worked Examples

Question
A glass block has a refractive index of 1.50. Calculate its critical angle.
Step 1: Formula
State the relationship between refractive index (n) and critical angle (c):
sin c = 1 / n
Step 2: Substitution
Substitute the given refractive index n = 1.50 into the formula:
sin c = 1 / 1.50 = 0.667
Step 3: Calculate Answer
Solve for c by taking the inverse sine (sin⁻¹):
c = sin⁻¹(0.667) ≈ 41.8° (to 3 s.f.)
Question
A certain liquid has a critical angle of 45.0°. Determine its refractive index.
Step 1: Formula
State the relationship between refractive index (n) and critical angle (c):
n = 1 / sin c
Step 2: Substitution
Substitute c = 45.0° into the equation:
n = 1 / sin(45.0°)
Step 3: Calculate Answer
Perform the calculation using a scientific calculator:
n = 1 / 0.7071 ≈ 1.41 (to 3 s.f.)
Optical Fibre Concept
Optical fibres transmit data using total internal reflection along a thin glass core wrapped in a cladding of lower refractive index.
Important Rule
For TIR to happen repeatedly inside the core, the angle of incidence at the core-cladding boundary must always be greater than the critical angle (i > c), and the core must have a higher refractive index than the cladding (n_core > n_cladding).

Definition

Critical Angle

Answer

The angle of incidence in a optically denser medium for which the angle of refraction in the less dense medium is 90°.
