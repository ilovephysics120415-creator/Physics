Refraction & Snell's Law

Concept Introduction
Light bends when it changes medium.
Interactive Exploration
Total Internal Reflection! Light is trapped inside the denser medium.
Refractive Index: n = sin θ1 sin θ2 (θ1 in air, θ2 in medium)
Angle in Air (θ₁) 30.0°
Angle in Medium (θ₂) 22.1°
Refractive Index (n) 1.33

Worked Examples
Problem Statement
Light travels from Air into Glass (n = 1.50) at an angle of incidence of 40.0° in air. Find the angle of refraction in glass.
O-Level Syllabus Formula
n = sin θair sin θmedium
Substitution & Calculation
1.50 = sin(40.0°) / sin(θglass)
sin(θglass) = sin(40.0°) / 1.50 = 0.6428 / 1.50 = 0.4285
θglass = sin⁻¹(0.4285) ≈ 25.4°
Problem Statement
A ray of light enters a transparent block from air at an incident angle of 50.0°. The angle of refraction is measured as 30.7°. Find the refractive index of the block.
O-Level Syllabus Formula
n = sin i sin r
Substitution & Calculation
n = sin(50.0°) / sin(30.7°)
n = 0.7660 / 0.5105
n ≈ 1.50
Problem Statement
A light ray exits a diamond (n = 2.42) into water (n = 1.33). The refracted angle in water is 35.0°. What was the angle of incidence inside the diamond?
O-Level Syllabus Relationship
sin θwater sin θdiamond = ndiamond nwater
Substitution & Calculation
sin(35.0°) / sin(θdiamond) = 2.42 / 1.33 = 1.820
sin(θdiamond) = sin(35.0°) / 1.820 = 0.5736 / 1.820 = 0.3152
θdiamond = sin⁻¹(0.3152) ≈ 18.4°
