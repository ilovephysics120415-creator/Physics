Radiation Hazards & Safety Protocols Reference

IONISING RADIATION

UV, X-rays & Gamma Rays

These high-frequency waves carry enough energy per photon to remove tightly bound electrons from atoms, creating ions. In biological tissues, this directly damages DNA molecules or creates reactive free radicals, potentially triggering mutations or cellular death.

  • Hazards: Severe sunburns, cell mutation, tissue destruction, cancer.
  • Safety Protocols: Lead shielding, minimize exposure time, protective clothing/goggles, maximizing distance (inverse-square law).
THERMAL RADIATION

Infrared & Microwaves

These waves do not have enough energy to ionise molecules, but their frequencies match the vibrational or rotational modes of chemical bonds (especially water molecules in microwaves). The absorbed energy is converted into kinetic thermal energy, heating the tissue.

  • Hazards: Internal tissue heating, skin burns, cataracts (due to heating of the eye lens).
  • Safety Protocols: Shielding enclosures (e.g., metal mesh in microwave doors), remote operation, limiting continuous exposure.
LOW RISK RADIATION

Radio & Visible Waves

Visible light is generally low risk under normal levels but can cause thermal eye damage under high intensity (like lasers). Radio waves are extremely low energy and present minimal hazard under standard domestic exposure limits.

  • Hazards: Minimal hazard; high intensity laser beams can cause localized burns or retinal damage.
  • Safety Protocols: Avoid looking directly at high-power light sources; laser safety goggles.
