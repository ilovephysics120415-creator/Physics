Pressure Foundations

P = F ÷ A
Pressure = Force divided by Area. Unit: Pascal (Pa) = N/m²

Hydraulic Systems

P = F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂
Pascal's Principle: pressure is transmitted equally throughout an enclosed fluid.
F₂ = F₁ × (A₂ ÷ A₁)
Force multiplication based on the ratio of piston areas.

Liquid Pressure & Density

ρ = m ÷ V
Density = mass divided by volume. Standard SI unit: kg/m³.
P = hρg
Liquid Pressure = depth × density × gravitational field strength (g = 10 N/kg).

Atmospheric Pressure & Manometers

Pₐ = hρg
Barometer pressure. Mercury density (ρ) = 13,600 kg/m³.
Pgas = Pₐ + hρg
Manometer with gas pressure ABOVE atmospheric pressure.
Pgas = Pₐ - hρg
Manometer with gas pressure BELOW atmospheric pressure.
