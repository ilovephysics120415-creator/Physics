Compass & Field Direction

A compass is simply a small bar magnet that is free to rotate.

When placed in a magnetic field, the compass needle's North (N) pole points in the direction of the magnetic field at that specific point.

Single Bar Magnet Pattern

Magnetic field lines form closed loops around a magnet:

  • Lines exit the North (N) pole, curve around, and enter the South (S) pole.
  • Lines are dense (closer together) near the poles, indicating a stronger magnetic field.
  • Lines spread out and grow further apart as distance increases.

Unlike Poles (Attraction)

When unlike poles (N and S) face each other, the magnetic field lines **join directly** from the North pole of one magnet to the South pole of the other.

This creates a strong, direct path representing an **attractive force** drawing them together.

Like Poles (Repulsion)

When like poles (N and N, or S and S) face each other, the field lines **bend away and repel** each other.

This creates a **Neutral Point (𝘟)** in the exact center where the magnetic fields cancel out completely. The net magnetic field strength at a neutral point is **zero**.
