Magnetism
1. Properties of Magnets

How do magnets behave?

  • Attract magnetic materials (iron, steel, nickel, cobalt).
  • Have two poles: North (N) and South (S).
  • Like poles repel; unlike poles attract.
  • A magnetic field exists all around a magnet.
2. Induced Magnetism

Poles are created instantly

  • Placement near a strong magnet or inside a solenoid **magnetises** a material.
  • The nearest induced pole is **always opposite** to the inducing pole.
  • This opposite pole always results in a force of **attraction**.
3. Temporary vs Permanent

Iron vs Steel

Iron (Temporary)

  • Magnetised easily
  • Loses magnetism easily
  • Electromagnets, Relays

Steel (Permanent)

  • Harder to magnetise
  • Retains magnetism
  • Compasses, fridge magnets
